Can you hear sounds in space?

No, space is a vacuum, which means it lacks the air or other mediums necessary for sound to travel. In space, there is complete silence as we traditionally understand it.

What do astronauts eat in space?

Astronauts eat a variety of foods specially prepared and packaged for space travel. These foods include freeze-dried fruits and vegetables, vacuum-sealed meat, and rehydratable dishes. The food must have a long shelf life and be easy to prepare in microgravity.

Can you grow plants in space?

Yes, plants can be grown in space using specialized growth chambers and hydroponic systems. NASA and other space agencies conduct experiments to study how plants grow and adapt to the unique conditions of microgravity.

What is the "overview effect"?

The "overview effect" is a term used to describe the profound shift in perspective that some astronauts experience when they see Earth from space. They often describe a deep sense of connection to the planet and a heightened environmental awareness.
